1. Taj Mahal, Agra

Taj Mahal, a UNESCO Worls Heritage Site, is one of the most famous historical places in Agra city of Uttar Pradesh and should be visited at least once in a life time. It represents the pinnacle of grand Mughal architecture and is built entirely of white marble. Shah Jahan built this magnificent monument for one of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

Intresting Fact About Taj Mahal: There is a shopping complex and hotel being built in Dubai which is inspired by the Taj Mahal. It's estimated to be four times larger in size than the original Taj Mahal.

2. Chittor Fort, Chittorgarh

Previously the capital of Mewar, Chittor Fort or Chittorgarh Fort is located in Rajasthan and is a fine example of the fine Rajput architecture. This imposing structure is one of the largest forts in India and among India's top historical sites. It was featured in the Bollywood movie 'Padmaavat', which gained this fort a lot more popularity among history lovers.

Intresting Facts About Chittor Fort: This majestic fort was built by the Mauryands in the 7th century and thus, it gets its name from the mauryan ruler, Chitranganda Mori.

3. Humayun's Tomb, Delhi

One of Delhi's historical jewels, Humayun's Tomb is a must-visit historical place for tourists in India. Surrounded by manicured lawns, this massive tomb is a wonderful monument known for its Mughal architecture and the timeless saga of love and longing. Built by Humayun's first wife, Empress Haji Begum in the loving memory of her husband, the tomb houses graves of both emperor and his wife as a testament of their eternal love.

Intresting Fact About Humayun's Tomb: It is the first garden tomb built in India and has not only two but 100 graves inside the single complex.

5. Gwalior Fort, Madhya Pradesh

If you truly love to visit and explore the ancient monuments of India, Gwalior Fort is a must-visit place. An architectural marvel of the 8th century, Gwalior Fort is reputes to be one of the most beautiful fortresses in the country and is a thriving tourist destination in Madhya Pradesh. Much to the pride of the locals, this magnificent fort has become a place of worship, wonder and sheer amazement for tourists all around the world.

Intresting Facts About Gwalior Fort: Gwalior Fort is the place where the second oldest record of number "zero" was found in one of the temples inside the fort.

6. Mysore Palace, Mysore

Mysore Palace is built in Indo-Saracenic style of architecture and is a magnificent edifice in Karnataka. Mysore Palace is also known as the Amba Vilas Palace, which was the former residence of the royal family of Mysore and it is still. Counted among the biggest palaces in India, the facade of this marvellous palace is a harmonious blend of Hindu, Muslim, Rajput and Gothic styles, which imparts it a regal quality.

Intresting Facts About Mysore Palace: Mysore Palace is the second most visited destination of India after Taj Mahal.

7. Hawa Mahal, Jaipur

Hawa Mahal, or the Palace of Winds, was more of a gallery than a monument. It was designed by Lal Chand Usthad as a gallery from where ladies of the royal household could watch the everyday life in the street, since at other times, they had to observe the purdah. Built in red and pink sandstone, it is shaped like a crown as the ruler of Jaipur during that time.

Intresting Facts About Hawa Mahal: Hawa Mahal is the tallest building in the world without a proper foundation. It consists of 5 stories, but, without a proper foundation, leans at an angle of 87 degrees.
